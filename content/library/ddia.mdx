Reading this slowly, one chapter at a time, and letting each one settle before moving on. Every chapter has directly changed how I think about Prism's architecture.

Chapter 11 on stream processing was the catalyst for rebuilding around event sourcing. The distinction between derived data and system of record clarified a lot of fuzzy thinking about where truth lives in an event-driven system.

Currently on Part III — the chapters on consistency and consensus. Dense but essential. Kleppmann has a rare ability to make formally precise ideas feel intuitive.
