The core insight — that large-scale human cooperation depends on shared fictions (money, nations, corporations) — is one of those ideas that's obvious in retrospect but reorganises your thinking once you see it.

Software systems are imagined orders too. APIs, protocols, data formats — they're shared fictions that enable coordination between machines and teams. They work because everyone agrees to treat them as real.

Less convinced by the later chapters on happiness and the future, but Part I on the cognitive revolution is exceptional.
